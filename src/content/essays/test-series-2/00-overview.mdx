<span class="dropcap">T</span>his is the second test series, and it demonstrates a different archival pattern from `test-series`. Look at the `/archive` page: this overview is listed once, but the inner parts are not. The reader discovers them by clicking through — first into this overview, then via the sidebar on the left or the **Next →** link at the bottom of each page.

This is how Leopold Aschenbrenner structures *Situational Awareness* on his site: a single entry, several pages of reading.

<Pullquote>
The archive shows you the door. The essay shows you the rooms.
</Pullquote>

## How it works

Every part of the series carries `series: "test-series-2"` in its frontmatter — that's what wires up the sidebar and pager. The two inner parts also carry one extra flag:

```yaml
unlisted: true
```

That flag means: still build a URL for this page, still include it in the series sidebar, but don't list it in the archive or on the home page. The overview (this page) does **not** have `unlisted: true`, so it appears in the archive on its own.

When you start the next page using the link below, you'll see the sidebar carry over and the pager swap to point further along.
